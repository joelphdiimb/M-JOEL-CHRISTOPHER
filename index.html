About

I am a Doctoral Candidate in Finance & Accounting at the Indian Institute of Management Bangalore, on the job market in 2026. My research focuses on banking, financial intermediation, and corporate finance, with an emphasis on policy-relevant questions in emerging markets. My work has received multiple Best Paper Awards and the Mirae Asset Scholar Award (2025). Research

Job Market Paper

Banking Against the Odds: Performance and Lending Efficiency of Government Banks during Crisis

with Sankarshan Basu, Abhinav Anand, and Nitin Vishen

Government-owned banks are often inefficient but can stabilize economies during downturns. Using difference-in-differences analyses of Indian banks during a nationwide economic crisis, we show that government-owned banks expanded lending, especially in severely impacted regions. However, contrary to prior literature, these banks outperformed private banks in terms of stock market returns by significantly improving profitability as well as lending quality. We attribute this improvement in performance to pre-pandemic policy interventions, including the adoption of digital banking and regulator-driven balance sheet clean-ups, which enabled sustainable counter-cyclical lending. Non-banking activities, deposit growth, and bank mergers do not explain the observed increases in lending and profitability. Cross-country analyses of the same global shock show that greater government ownership of banking assets is associated with larger lending increases and, conditional on pre-crisis loan-quality improvements, higher post-crisis profitability.

Target: The Review of Financial Studies
